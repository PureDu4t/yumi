I've always dreamed of designing audio games where player location can be precisely told. Over the years, I have come up with a working navigation system using audio and "I Closed My Eyes" was my first attempt in implementing that. The game uses a sfx navigation system, where horizontal and vertical location is represented by one pitch followed by another delayed pitch. The pitches ascend and descent according to the pentatonic scale. This was done to avoid dissonance and make player movement sound like a melody on top of the underscore, which is groove oriented and has no melody. In addition to the location navigation system, we designed the "blind cane," which allows the player to hear the surrounding 8 tiles in clockwise motion. The quality of the tiles are represented with sound effects. For example, if there is a ghost on the tile, the player would hear "ooo."
